CHAPTER 2: ACIDS, BASES AND SALTS.




EXPERIMENT 3:REACTION BETWEEN ACIDS AND BASES.


AIM:To carry out the reaction between acid and a base.


MATERIALS REQUIRED: Test tube/beaker, dropper, Phenolphthalein indicator, Solution of sodium hydroxide (NaOH) and Dilute Hydrochloric acid (Dil. HCl)


PROCEDURE:
• Take about 2 mL solution of NaOH solution (Sodium Hydroxide) in a test tube/beaker.
• Add a drop of phenolphthalein indicator to it and observe the color.
• Add Dil. HCl dropwise with the help of a dropper and stir the solution constantly until the color disappears.
• Add a few drops of NaOH solution and observe the change.


OBSERVATION:
• Pink color solution is observed when a drop of phenolphthalein is added to the solution of NaOH.
• The pink color fades on adding HCl.
• This is due to reaction between HCl and NaOH.
• The solution becomes colorless when NaOH has reacted completely with HCl.
• The solution turns pink again on addition of NaOH.


INFERENCE:
• When Dilute HCl is added to NaOH solution, there is reaction between them.
• When the required amount of the acid i.e., Dilute HCl is added, the basic properties and the acidic properties of NaOH and Dil. HCl disappear respectively.
• This process is called Neutralization and results in the formation of Salt and water.
• The reaction between HCl and NaOH forms NaCl (Sodium chloride) and water.
• HCl (aq) + NaOH (aq) → NaCl (aq) + H2O (l)
• In general, the reaction between an acid and a base can be written as:
• Acid + Base → Salt + Water


PRECAUTIONS:
• Care must be taken while handling the acid and base solutions.
• All the setup must be properly done.
• Safety gloves should be used.
• Tasting or touching the solution should not be done.


ADDITIONAL INFORMATION:
• Another example of neutralization reaction is:
• Sulphuric acid and Potassium Hydroxide react to form potassium sulphate and water.
• H2SO4(aq) + 2KOH (aq) → K2SO4(aq) + 2H2O (l)
