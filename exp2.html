EXPERIMENT 2 :COMBINATION REACTION


AIM:To carry out the reaction of between quick lime (Calcium oxide, CaO) and water to classify it as a combination reaction.


MATERIALS REQUIRED: Calcium oxide, Watch glass, Jet Tube, Beaker, Test tube and distilled water.


PROCEDURE: • Take a small amount of Calcium Oxide (Quick lime) using a spatula and to a beaker.
• Slowly add distilled water into the beaker. Pour enough water so that the quick lime remains submerged in the water.
• Here quick lime reacts with water and forms a suspension of slaked lime in water.
• CaO(s) + H2O(l) Ca(OH)2 + Heat
(Quick lime) (Slaked Lime)
• This reaction is exothermic in nature as it releases thermal energy in the process.
• Now keep the beaker undisturbed for some time.
• We can see that a clear solution appears as the suspension of slaked lime settles down.
• This clear solution is known as lime water.
• To confirm the presence of calcium hydroxide in lime water, add a few drops of clear solution i.e., lime water into a boiling tube.
• Close the mouth of the boiling with a cork and insert a glass tube into it.
• Then blow the exhaled air into the lime water through this glass tube.
• On blowing, carbon dioxide contained in the exhaled air turns lime water milky.


INFERENCE: • This is a combination reaction as the two reactants- quick lime and water react to form a single product, calcium hydroxide i.e., slaked lime.
• Exhaled air contains CO2 which turns lime water milky.


PRECAUTIONS:
• Ensure quick lime does not come in contact with moist skin as it can hurt the skin.
• Always pour water into a small quantity of quick lime taken in a beaker.
• Never put quick lime directly into the water as it may cause water to splash out.
• Avoid inhalation of quick lime as it may cause coughing, sneezing etc,


ADDITIONAL INFORMATION:
A solution of slaked lime is used for whitewashing walls. Calcium hydroxide reacts slowly with the carbon dioxide in air to form a thin layer of calcium carbonate on the walls. Calcium carbonate is formed after two to three days of whitewashing and gives A shiny finish to the walls. It is interesting to note that the chemical formula for marble is also CaCO3.
Ca(OH)2 (aq) + CO2(g) CaCO3 (s) + H2O(l)
