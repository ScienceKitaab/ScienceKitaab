CHAPTER 2: ACIDS, BASES AND SALTS.




EXPERIMENT 2:REACTION OF METAL CARBONATES AND METAL HYDROGEN CARBONATES WITH ACIDS.


AIM: To carry out the reaction between metal carbonates and metal hydrogen carbonates with acid.


MATERIALS REQUIRED: Sodium Bicarbonate (NaHCO3), Sodium Carbonate (Na2CO3), Dilute Hydrochloric acid (Dil. HCl), Calcium Hydroxide (Lime water-Ca (OH)2), Boiling test tubes, Cork, Stand, Blue Litmus paper, Delivery tube.


PROCEDURE:
• Take two boiling test tubes. Add NaHCO3 in one test tube and Na2CO3 in the other using a spatula.
• Add a little amount of Dil. HCl to the test tubes. A brisk effervescence is observed in both the test tubes.
• Blue litmus paper turns red when it is exposed to the gas coming out of the reaction.
• Preparation of Lime water:
• Add Ca (OH)2 (s) to water using spatula and stir it well.
• Then filter this water using a filter paper and funnel into another test tube. The water filtered is Lime water.
•Passing the gas through Calcium Hydroxide Solution:
• After adding Dil. HCl to NaHCO3 and Na2CO3, immediately close the mouth of the test tube with cork having a delivery tube.
• Keep the delivery tube in the test tube containing lime water and observe.


OBSERVATION AND INFERENCE:
• Production of Carbon dioxide (CO2) gives rise to the effervescence when NaHCO3 and Na2CO3 react with Dil. HCl.
• Na2CO3 (s) + 2HCl(aq) → 2NaCl (aq) + H2O (l) + CO2 (g)
• NaHCO3 (s) + HCl(aq) → NaCl (aq) + H2O (l) + CO2 (g)
• Blue litmus paper turns red because Carbon dioxide (CO2) is acidic in nature.
• If we bring a burning match stick close to the test tube after the reaction, Carbon dioxide (CO2) extinguishes the fire as it does not support combustion.
• Carbon dioxide (CO2) turns Lime water (Ca (OH)2) milky when passed through it.
• Ca (OH)2 (aq) + CO2 (g) → CaCO3 (s) + H2O (l)
• All metal carbonates and hydrogen carbonates react with acids to give a corresponding salt, carbon dioxide and water.
• Metal carbonate/ Metal hydrogen carbonates + Acid → Salt + Carbon Dioxide + Water


PRECAUTIONS:
• Always wear safety goggles and gloves.
• Be careful while handing chemicals like Ca (OH)2 as it may be toxic.
