CHAPTER 1: CHEMICAL REACTIONS AND EQUATIONS.




EXPERIMENT 7: DISPLACEMENT REACTION.


AIM: In this experiment we observe a reaction between iron and copper sulphate solution and classify it.


MATERIALS REQUIRED: Copper sulphate solution, Iron nails, boiling test tubes, Stands with clamp and thread.


PROCEDURE:
• Take two stands with clamps and fix one test tube in each stand.
• Tie one iron nail with a piece of thread and hang it in one of the test tubes and tie the other end of the thread to the stand.
• Take copper sulphate solution in a beaker and pour it into the both test tubes.
• Keep the apparatuses undisturbed for a day.
• After one day, remove the iron nail immersed in the solution and compare it with the other iron nail which wasn’t immersed in the copper sulphate solution.


OBSERVATION:
• Iron nail which was dipped in the copper sulphate solution shows a brown coating compared to the greyish color of the other nail.
• The color of the copper sulphate solution in the test tube in which the nail has been dipped changes from blue to green and a bit colorless.
• The color of the copper sulphate solution in which nail wasn’t dipped stays the same color.


INFERENCE:
• The brown coating indicates that the copper is deposited on the iron nail by displacing iron.
• The change of colour from blue to green of the solution indicates a chemical change in which Copper has been displaced by iron from the copper sulphate solution and a new compound Ferrous sulphate (FeSO4) is formed.
• CuSO4 (aq) + Fe (s) → FeSO4 + Cu (s)
• Since there is displacement of one chemical with another this reaction can be classified as a displacement reaction


PRECAUTIONS:
• Try to avoid touching the copper sulphate solution and the nail dipped in the solution.
• It is advised to wear gloves while using materials containing copper sulphate.


ADDITIONAL INFORMATION:
• The chemical reaction in which a more reactive element displaces a less reactive element from its compound is known as a displacement reaction.
• Both metals and non-metals take part in the displacement reactions.
• Other examples of displacement reactions are
• Zn (s) + CuSO4 (aq) → ZnSO4 (aq) + Cu (s)
• Pb (s) + CuCl2 (aq) → PbCl2 (aq) + Cu (s)
