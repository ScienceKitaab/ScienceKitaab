CHAPTER 2: ACIDS, BASES AND SALTS.




EXPERIMENT 4:REACTION OF ACIDS WITH METAL OXIDES


AIM:To carry out the reaction of acids with metal oxides.


MATERIALS REQUIRED: Beaker/test tube, Glass rod, copper oxide and Dilute Hydrochloric acid.


PROCEDURE:
• Take little amount of black copper oxide in a beaker/test tube.
• Add about 10 mL of Dilute hydrochloric acid.
• With the help of a glass rod stir the solution gently.
• As the reaction is happening observe the beaker/test tube.


OBSERVATION:
• When a mixture of copper oxide and Dilute HCl is mixed, suspended black particles of copper oxide can be seen in the colorless solution of dilute hydrochloric acid.
• The black particles slowly dissolve as the reaction proceeds.
• Due to the formation of a salt i.e., copper (II) chloride (cupric chloride), the solution becomes bluish green.


INFERENCE:
• Formation of copper (II) chloride (cupric chloride) is the result of the reaction between copper oxide and dilute hydrochloric acid.
• It is a salt of copper and forms bluish green solution.
• The reaction is:
CuO (s) + 2HCl (aq) → CuCl2 (aq) + H2O (l)
• The general reaction between metal oxides and acids is
Metal oxide + Acid → Salt + Water


PRECAUTIONS:
• Care must be taken while handling the acid and base solutions.
• All the setup must be properly done.
• Safety gloves should be used.
• Tasting or touching the solution should not be done.


ADDITIONAL INFORMATION:
Other metal oxides like calcium oxide (CaO) or quick lime and magnesium oxide (MgO) also react in a similar way with acids.
• CaO (s) + 2HCl (aq) → CaCl2 (aq) + H2O (l)
