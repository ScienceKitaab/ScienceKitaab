EXPERIMENT 3:DECOMPOSITION REACTION.


AIM: Finding the action of heat on Ferrous Sulphate crystals and further classifying the reaction.


MATERIALS REQUIRED: Ferrous Sulphate crystals, Boiling tubes, Test tube holder and a spatula, Bunsen burner.


PROCEDURE: • Using a spatula and transfer a slight quantity of Ferrous sulphate crystals into a dry boiling tube.
• Note the light green colour of the ferrous sulphate crystals.
• Hold the boiling test tube with the holder and heat it over Bunsen burners Flame for a large time.
• While heating Note the odour by smelling the fumes coming out of the boiling tube.
• Finally transfer the heated substance in the test tube into a watch glass.

OBSERVATION: The crystal’s colour changes from light green to white. Further heating changes the white substance to dark brown solid.



INFERENCE: • The colour change is due to the formation of anhydrous Ferrous sulphate which is produced when the Ferrous sulphate crystals are heated.
• FeSO4.7H2O --Heat--> FeSO4 + 7H2O
• Ferrous sulphate becomes Anhydrous due to the loss of water molecules on heating the crystals.
• Then it decomposes to form a dark brown solid i.e., Ferric oxide and Sulphur dioxide and sulphur trioxide which are emitted out of the test tube as gases.
2FeSO4(s) --Heat--> Fe2O3 (s) + SO2(g) +SO3(g)
• From the observation, we can understand that this is a decomposition reaction because the single reactant, Anhydrous Ferrous sulphate is breaking down to give much simple products like Ferric oxide, Sulphur dioxide and Sulphur trioxide.


PRECAUTIONS:
• While heating a test tube make sure to always us a test tube holder.
• Inhalation of the gases i.e., SO3 and SO2 must be avoided as difficulty in breathing may arise.


ADDITIONAL INFORMATION:
Another example is decomposition of Calcium Carbonate (CaCO3) to Calcium Oxide and Carbon dioxide on heating. This is an important reaction used in various industries like in the manufacture of cement. As this reaction occurs through heating it is called Thermal Decomposition.
CaCO3 (s) --Heat--> CaO(s) + CO2 (g)
