CHAPTER 1: CHEMICAL REACTIONS AND EQUATIONS.




EXPERIMENT 4: ELECTROLYTIC DECOMPOSITION REACTION (ELECTROLYSIS)


AIM: Observation of the electrolysis of water and determining the gases liberated in the process.


MATERIALS REQUIRED: Mini Hoffman apparatus (Electrolysis unit, Electrical wire with alligator clips (2 wires), Gas collection tubes), 6-Volt or 9-Volt battery, Salt or NaOH solution), Water, stirring rod, Matches or candle.


BACKGROUND:
Electrolysis of water if the process through which water is decomposed into Hydrogen and Oxygen gas, when electric current is passed through it. For the electrolyte i.e., we add salt (highly polar) or NaOH solution so that the ions of salt or NaOH pull the water molecules apart into ions. Water molecule is decomposed into H+ and OH- ions. The ions move to the oppositely charged electrodes and liberated as gases at the electrodes. Bubbles of Hydrogen gas(H2) is formed at the cathode and the Bubbles of oxygen (O2) form at the anode. The amount of hydrogen gas produced is twice that of oxygen gas. The reaction is
2H2O (l) --> 2H2(g) + O2(g)


PROCEDURE:
The apparatus consists of two electrodes (Graphite or Platinum) on which the discharge tube or test tube is placed upside down after filling the apparatus with electrolyte. To the water the alligator clips of wire are connected to a 6V battery one to the positive and the other negative terminal (Electrodes behave as Anode and Cathode respectively) and then the current is sent through the water. After some time, H+ and OH- ions move to the electrodes which are oppositely charged. The gases collected during the process can be tested with a glowing splint and the observations are recorded .


OBSERVATION AND INFERENCE:
• In one glass tube the amount of gas collected is twice the amount in the other glass tube.
• When a glowing splint is kept near the tube which collected twice the amount of gas, Ignition and an audible pop can be observed. Indicating the presence of Hydrogen
• The glowing splint kept near the other tube will flame up more indicating the presence of Oxygen.


CHEMICAL REACTION:
• Anode (+ve electrode): 2H2O(l) --> O2 (g) + 4H+(aq)
• Cathode (-ve electrode): 2H+(aq) + 2e ---> H2(g)
• Overall reaction: 2H2O (l) --> 2H2(g) + O2(g)


PRECAUTIONS:
• Be careful of explosive hydrogen and oxygen mixtures.
• It is a good idea to burn the gases off to prevent a dangerous mixture.
• Always wear goggles and safety gloves while performing the experiment.
• Should be done under the guidance of a teacher.


ADDITIONAL INFORMATION:
• This electrolysis can be done without the Hoffman Apparatus using beaker, rubber stopper and graphite rods as electrodes but care and safety precautions must be taken accordingly.
• Another example of Electrolytic decomposition is
• Decomposition of sodium chloride: On passing electricity through molten sodium chloride, it decomposes into sodium and chlorine.
• 2NaCl --electricity--> 2Na + Cl2
